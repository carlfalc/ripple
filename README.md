# Ripple

Ripple is a mobile app that helps college students and young professionals discover their peers' favorite spots through authentic photo check-ins and micro-reviews. Think Foursquare meets BeReal for local discovery. Users share honest 30-second video reviews of cafes, bars, study spots, and hangouts, creating hyperlocal discovery networks within university towns and young professional areas.

## About

Young people aged 18-28 struggle to discover authentic local spots in new cities or even their own university towns. They're tired of generic Google reviews from random demographics and want recommendations from people like them. International students especially feel lost trying to find the cool study cafe or affordable lunch spot that locals love. Current discovery apps are either too broad (Google Maps) or too curated by influencers (Instagram). Students waste hours scrolling through irrelevant reviews or end up at tourist traps instead of genuine local gems where their peers actually hang out.

## Tech Stack

I scored 8 because: (1) Standard React Native app with video upload/streaming - well-established patterns, (2) Location services and mapping APIs are mature and affordable, (3) Content moderation can start manual then scale with AI tools. Only complexity is real-time 'vibe check' features but that's optional for MVP. Can build solid MVP in 8-10 weeks.

## Point of Difference

Age-gated community (18-28 only) ensures relevant peer recommendations. 30-second video format prevents fake reviews while being more authentic than photos. University and workplace verification creates trusted micro-communities. AI matching suggests spots based on your actual preferences, not just popularity. Real-time 'vibe check' feature shows how busy/social places are right now.

## Target Users

Primary: University students (18-24) exploring campus towns, international students seeking local integration, young professionals (22-28) new to cities. Secondary: Local businesses wanting authentic student/young professional marketing. Personas include Emma (international student, lonely, wants to find study spots), Jake (young professional, moved for work, seeks weekend activities), Sarah (local student, wants to share hidden gems).
